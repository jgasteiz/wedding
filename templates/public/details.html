{% trans "Polish weddings, what to expect" %}

{% trans "Eat, Dance, Drink, Repeat (at least 5 times)" %}

{% trans "The party starts with food (probably chicken soup) and it's followed by some meat dish. That's the standard." %} {% trans "After that, you should expect the First Dance and then the vodka will start flowing." %}

{% trans "What food should I expect?" %}

{% trans "Traditionally, Polish weddings start with a traditional chicken soup. It's fatty and good for making a good pre-drinking base in your stomach." %} {% trans "After that, you should expect around 4 or 5 main dishes of meat and salads every couple of hours." %} {% trans "These dishes will probably be served between 6pm and 2am, being the last one most likely beetroot soup with dumplings (also traditional dish)." %}

{% trans "If you are vegetarian/vegan or have some kind of food intolerance, please get in touch with me and we'll sort something out." %}

{% trans "How much should I eat?" %}

{% trans "As much as you want. The more you eat, the more you can drink." %}

{% trans "What should I drink?" %}

{% trans "Vodka! But do not mix it with other alcohol or people (me included) will start taking pictures of you." %} {% trans "There will be beer, but only drink it if you're not planning to drink vodka. Seriously, this is a good advice." %} {% trans "There might be some wine, but it won't be nice Spanish wine." %}

{% trans "And of course, there will be plenty of juices, water and other soft drinks." %}

{% trans "How much should I drink?" %}

{% trans "I've only experienced one Polish wedding in my life and I survived it quite gracefully." %} {% trans "If you're not Polish, I suggest you fill half of your shot glass. It worked well for me." %}

{% trans "How much should I dance?" %}

{% trans "As much as possible. The more you dance, the better chances you'll have to survive and enjoy the wedding." %}
