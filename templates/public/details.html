{% trans "Polish weddings, what to expect" %}

{% trans "Eat, Dance, Drink, Repeat (5 times)" %}

{% blocktrans %}The party starts with a traditional chicken soup and it's followed by a meat dish. That's the standard. After that, you should expect the First Dance and then the vodka will start flowing.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "What food will be served?" %}

{% blocktrans %}Traditionally, Polish weddings start with a traditional chicken soup. Nowadays it's not necessarily to have it, but I'm all up for traditions. After that, you should expect around 4 or 5 main dishes of meat and salads every couple of hours. These dishes will be served between 5pm and 2am, being the last one a delicious beetroot soup with dumplings – a very traditional one too.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "How much should I eat?" %}

{% blocktrans %}As much as you can! If you've been invited to this wedding is because you're our friend or family. And if that's the case, you must love food.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "What drinks will be served?" %}

{% blocktrans %}If you're used to Spanish weddings, this one might seem a bit different. There might be some wine, but won't be the main alcoholic drink of the wedding. Vodka will.{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}Of course, you can mix the vodka with anything you like, but you'll see the most of the locals drinking it in shots. And I encourage you to try the same. We're personally making sure we're getting one of the best – if not the best – Polish vodkas, so people can drink it without worrying too much about the next morning. Our only advice would be to take it easy if you're planning to drink other alcohols, everyone knows what happens when you mix too much right?{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}That said, there will be plenty of beer, juices, water and other soft drinks.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "How much should I dance?" %}

{% blocktrans %}As much as you can! Dancing is a very social and common thing to do in a Polish wedding, everyone must dance.{% endblocktrans %}
