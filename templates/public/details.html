{% trans "What do I need to take with me?" %}

{% trans "Passport or National ID" %}

{% blocktrans %}Poland is part of the EU, which means there's no need to take the passport with you, but I recommend you do it, if you have one. Everything will be faster for you in the airport and if you lose one of your ids, you still have a backup one.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Money" %}

{% blocktrans %}Get around 100 or 200 złoty from your local bank, just to have some cash with you. You will need cash for paying buses, trains, taxis and beer in the bars. You won't be able to pay with card in many bars and restaurants.{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}There will be a good money exchange office in Przeworsk where you can easily exchange more pounds or euros into złoty. You can also take złoty directly from any ATM, which is what I do most of the times. Your bank might give you a worse exchange rate, but you won't have to carry a lot of money around.{% endblocktrans %}
